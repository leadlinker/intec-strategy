The Problems

Now that we know what is there, here is what those findings reveal about what is costing you leads right now.

Problem 1

The Website Does Not Match The Factory

Intec is an AS9100, NADCAP, ISO 17025 accredited aerospace lab with manufacturing under the same roof, private equity backing, and a new growth-mandated CEO on the trade show circuit. The website is a static 2021-era Webflow brochure that tells none of that story. A program manager at a Tier 1 who Googles you after the Paris handshake sees less authority online than they saw in your booth.

Here is what the buyer journey looks like today:

1

Engineer Hears Intec Pitch at Paris or On LinkedIn

They are curious. They want to see case studies, qualification examples, program wins, a capabilities one-pager.

2

They Land on intecaero.com

One hero, two service tiles, a footer with a phone number and an RFQ email. No proof, no technical content, no gated asset, no retargeting pixel.

3

They Scroll for Case Studies or Programs

There are none. No Boeing program mention, no named primes, no specific qualification campaigns, no engineering blog.

4

They Leave, Nothing Captured

No email captured. No cookie placed for retargeting. No way to reach back out. Your CEO just paid to fly to Paris and the lead slipped through a brochure site.

The factory is world class. The front door is not.
Problem 2

There Is No Lead Capture, No Segmentation, No Qualification

One contact form, four fields, one inbox. Every inbound query, whether it is a curious grad student, a procurement coordinator asking about a small specimen run, or a senior materials engineer at a prime sourcing a multi-year qualification campaign, lands in the same place and gets triaged by hand.

The Engineer With a Real RFQ

Has no technical asset to download before they reach out. They talk to competitors with gated capability decks and walk away thinking those competitors are more serious.

The Program Manager Researching Quietly

Has no reason to give up an email. They browse, leave, and are lost forever. You cannot remarket to them, send them a case study next week, or warm them for next quarter's budget cycle.

The Sourcing Lead Building a Short List

Has no proof of specialization. Your credentials are listed in tiny text in the footer. Competitors present this credential stack on a dedicated page.

You are being evaluated by your website even when you do not know it.
Problem 3

The Outbound and Trade Show Work Is Leaking

intecaero.com

Michael is on the circuit. Paris Airshow was a real investment in time and travel. LinkedIn outreach to OEMs and primes is happening. None of that effort is converting into owned, nurtured pipeline because there is no back end to catch it.

What Should Be There

A LinkedIn Insight Tag and a Meta Pixel firing on every page so every curious engineer who visits after a trade show conversation can be retargeted on their feed for 30 days. A gated technical asset (qualification readiness checklist, specimen prep spec sheet, thermal protocol overview) so visitors have a reason to give you an email. An email nurture sequence so once they give you that email, they do not go cold while their budget cycle resets.

Every show booth, every Paris conversation, every LinkedIn DM lands visitors on a page that cannot remember them.

Suggested Lead Magnets

5 Ideas for Intec

Five ideas based on what Intec already has or could easily create:

1

"The Aerospace Composites Qualification Readiness Checklist"

A gated PDF checklist plus short technical addendum covering the 12 things a materials engineer needs to verify before starting a qualification campaign with an external lab (facility certs, specimen specs, environmental preconditioning, NDT protocols, data delivery format). Targets materials engineers, sourcing leads, and program managers at OEMs, primes, and Tier 1 suppliers who are about to spec a composite part or start a qualification program. Qualifies the lead by function and intent.

This is the lead magnet the example page is built around
2

"The AS9100 + NADCAP + ISO 17025 Supplier Vetting Guide"

A short buyer-facing guide explaining what each accreditation actually means, what questions to ask a lab before signing, and why most aerospace buyers misunderstand the difference. Targets procurement leads vetting composite suppliers for the first time.

3

"High-Load Composite Testing Spec Sheet: 5 lbf to 600K lbf"

A one-page technical reference with Intec's full testing capability envelope (loads, temperatures, NDT methods, specimen types) formatted as a PDF that engineers can reference during program design. Gated on email.

4

"The Composite Manufacturing Materials Library: Epoxies, BMI, Polyimides, and Metal Matrix"

A short engineer-facing reference on which materials suit which programs, with Intec's processing capabilities mapped to each.

5

"From Specimen to Production: Intec Program Case Studies"

A quarterly-updated gated collection of anonymized program walk-throughs demonstrating how Intec takes a customer from specimen testing through qualification through production build.
