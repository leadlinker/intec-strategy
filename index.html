Problem 1

Your Website Does Not Match The Caliber Of Your Factory

Intec is an AS9100, NADCAP, ISO 17025 accredited aerospace lab with composite manufacturing under the same roof, Cornerstone Capital backing, and a new growth-mandated CEO on the Paris Airshow circuit. When a VP Engineering at Spirit, a Qualification Manager at Collins, or a Program Lead at a new-space company runs their standard due diligence on you, they land on a static 2021-era Webflow brochure that tells none of that story.

Problem 2

You Have No Qualification Layer, No Segmentation, No Follow-Up

One contact form with four fields. Every inbound query lands in the same inbox, whether it is a curious grad student, a procurement coordinator asking about a small specimen run, or a Materials Director at a Tier 1 prime sourcing a multi-year qualification campaign. There is no way to prioritize, no way to segment, and no way to follow up automatically once the form fires.

The Engineer With A Real RFQ

Has no technical asset to download before they reach out. They talk to competitors with gated capability decks and walk away thinking those competitors are more serious.

The Program Manager Researching Quietly

Has no reason to give up an email. They browse the site, leave, and are lost forever. No retargeting on LinkedIn, no case study in their inbox next week, no way to stay warm through a 6-month budget cycle.

The Procurement Lead Building A Short List

Has no proof of specialization to put in their internal deck. Your accreditations are in tiny text in the footer. Competitors present the same credentials as dedicated capability pages and published qualification case studies.

Aerospace buying committees have 3 to 7 people across materials, quality, program, and procurement. A single contact form is not a pipeline, it is a bottleneck.
